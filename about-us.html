The Island

Discover what makes Rasdhoo Island a perfect diving destination

Paradise in the Heart of the Maldives

Rasdhoo is a small but vibrant island located in the North Ari Atoll of the Maldives. As the capital of Alif Alif Atoll, this picturesque destination combines authentic local culture with breathtaking natural beauty, making it an ideal base for divers seeking adventure beneath the waves.

With its pristine white sandy beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and laid-back atmosphere, Rasdhoo offers a perfect balance between diving excitement and peaceful island living. The island spans just 500 meters in diameter, creating an intimate environment where everything is within walking distance.

Strategic Location

Just 56km from Malé, accessible by speedboat (1.5 hours) or domestic flight (20 minutes)

Dive Paradise

Home to 15 world-class dive sites including the famous Hammerhead Point

Perfect Beaches

Enjoy pristine white sand beaches and crystal-clear turquoise waters ideal for relaxation

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know for your diving adventure in Rasdhoo

How do I get to Rasdhoo Island?

From Malé Airport, your guesthouse will organize the transfer to Rasdhoo Island. There are two speedboat transfers available daily - one in the morning and one in the afternoon. The scenic journey takes approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes across the beautiful turquoise waters of the Maldives. Alternatively, you can take a domestic flight (20 minutes) followed by a short boat transfer.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept both cash and card payments for your convenience. Please note that card payments include a 3.6% processing fee added to your total amount. We recommend bringing some cash (USD or EUR) as many local businesses prefer cash transactions, and it's often more convenient for smaller purchases around the island.

Can non-divers join the boat trips?

Absolutely! Whether you're traveling with friends, family, or partners, non-divers are welcome to join our diving boat trips. They can enjoy snorkeling sessions at the dive sites for an additional fee. This way, everyone can experience the incredible marine life of the Maldives, whether above or below the surface. We provide all necessary snorkeling equipment.

What currency is used in the Maldives?

The official currency in the Maldives is the Rufiyaa (MVR). However, US Dollars and Euros are widely accepted throughout the islands. For the best rates and convenience around Rasdhoo Island, you can exchange your euros or dollars for rufiyaas at local shops, banks, or directly at Malé Airport upon arrival.

Do you provide diving equipment?

Yes, we provide all necessary diving equipment including BCDs, regulators, wetsuits, masks, fins, and tanks. All our equipment is regularly serviced and maintained to the highest safety standards. If you prefer to use your own gear, you're welcome to bring it. We also offer equipment for snorkeling including masks, fins, and snorkels.

What should I bring for my diving experience?

Please bring your diving certification card, logbook, and any personal diving equipment you prefer to use. Don't forget sunscreen (reef-safe), a towel, and a change of clothes. We recommend bringing an underwater camera to capture the amazing marine life. If you have any medical conditions, please bring relevant documentation or medical clearance.

Can complete beginners dive with you?

Absolutely! We welcome divers of all levels, including complete beginners. We offer SSI Open Water courses and Discover Scuba Diving programs for those who want to try diving for the first time. Our experienced instructors will ensure you feel comfortable and safe throughout your underwater journey. We also have shallow, calm dive sites perfect for beginners.

When is the best time to dive in Rasdhoo?

Diving in Rasdhoo is excellent year-round! The best visibility and calmest conditions are typically from November to April (dry season). However, each season offers unique experiences - hammerhead sharks are more commonly seen from December to April, while manta rays are frequently spotted from May to November. Water temperature remains comfortable at 27-30°C throughout the year.
