Beginner Courses

Our beginner courses are perfect for those new to diving or with limited experience. These courses focus on building fundamental skills, confidence, and comfort in the underwater environment.

Age Requirements: 8+ years (Bubble Maker), 10+ years (Junior Open Water), 15+ years (Open Water)
Prerequisites: Basic swimming ability (200m swim, 10 min float for certification courses)
Duration: 1 day (Try Scuba) to 3-4 days (Open Water Certification)
Maximum Depth: 5-6m (Try Scuba), 12m (Junior Open Water), 18m (Open Water)

Advanced Courses

For certified divers looking to expand their skills and explore more challenging dive environments. Advanced courses introduce specialized diving techniques and deeper depths while building on your existing knowledge.

Age Requirements: 12+ years (Junior Advanced), 15+ years (Advanced)
Prerequisites: Open Water Diver certification (or equivalent)
Duration: 2-3 days (5 adventure dives)
Maximum Depth: 30m (depending on age and experience)

Specialty & Professional Courses

Specialty courses allow divers to develop expertise in specific areas of diving, while professional courses prepare you for a career in the diving industry. These courses provide in-depth knowledge and hands-on experience.

Age Requirements: 15+ years (most specialties), 18+ years (professional courses)
Prerequisites: Varies by course (typically Open Water or Advanced certification)
Duration: 1-2 days (specialties) to 2-4 weeks (professional)
Career Path: Dive Guide → Divemaster → Instructor → Course Director

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before starting your dive education

Do I need to know how to swim to take a diving course?

Yes, basic swimming skills are required for most diving courses. For SSI Open Water Diver certification, you need to be able to swim 200 meters without stopping and float/tread water for 10 minutes. However, our Try Scuba Diving experience has less stringent requirements.

What's the minimum age requirement for diving courses?

The minimum age varies by course: Bubble Maker is for children 8-10 years old, Junior Open Water Diver is for ages 10-14, and standard Open Water Diver certification is for ages 15 and up. All minors require parental/guardian consent.

What equipment do I need to bring for the courses?

All diving equipment is provided for our courses, including BCD, regulator, wetsuit, mask, fins, and tanks. We recommend bringing your own swimwear, towel, and sunscreen. If you have your own diving equipment, you're welcome to use it after our instructors verify it's in proper working condition.

How long does it take to get certified?

The Open Water Diver course typically takes 3-4 days to complete, including theory sessions, confined water training, and open water dives. Advanced courses usually take 2-3 days, while specialty courses vary from 1-2 days depending on the specialty. The Divemaster course is more comprehensive and takes 2-4 weeks.

Are there any medical restrictions for diving?

Yes, certain medical conditions may restrict diving activities. All participants must complete a medical questionnaire before diving. Conditions such as asthma, heart problems, epilepsy, and certain medications may require physician approval. We prioritize your safety, so please disclose all medical conditions.
